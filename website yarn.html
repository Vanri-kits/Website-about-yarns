Yarn Types Interactive Guide

Cotton

From: Cotton plant fibers

Texture: Soft, smooth, breathable

Best For: Summer tops, baby clothes, dishcloths, bags, home décor

Notes: Doesn't stretch much; great for warm climates.

Wool

From: Sheep fleece

Texture: Warm, slightly stretchy; can be soft or coarse

Best For: Sweaters, scarves, hats, gloves, winter items

Notes: Holds heat well and keeps shape.

Bamboo

From: Processed bamboo pulp

Texture: Silky, smooth, cool to the touch

Best For: Summer garments, lightweight tops, baby clothing, shawls

Notes: Naturally breathable and hypoallergenic.

Silk

From: Silkworm cocoons

Texture: Extremely soft, shiny, luxurious

Best For: Shawls, drapey tops, scarves, elegant accessories

Notes: Strong fiber with excellent drape.

Acrylic

From: Synthetic (plastic-based fiber)

Texture: Varies: soft to firm, smooth, lightweight

Best For: Blankets, sweaters, everyday wear, beginner projects

Notes: Affordable, easy to wash, many color options.

Mohair

From: Angora goat fleece

Texture: Fluffy, light, fuzzy “halo”

Best For: Sweaters, airy scarves, lace projects, mixed-yarn textures

Notes: Very warm but lightweight.

Angora

From: Angora rabbit fur

Texture: Extremely soft, plush, fuzzy

Best For: Gloves, hats, scarves, luxury accessories

Notes: Very warm; often blended due to delicacy.

Alpaca

From: Alpaca fleece (camelid family)

Texture: Silky, soft, smooth, warmer than wool

Best For: Sweaters, blankets, shawls, winter wear

Notes: Hypoallergenic and durable.
